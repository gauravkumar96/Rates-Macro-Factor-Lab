Key Inferences & Insights

📉

COVID-Era Yield Curve Steepening

During 2020-2021, the term spread (10Y - 91D) widened dramatically to 2.64% on average — the steepest in the entire 15-year period. RBI slashed repo to 4.00%, pulling short-term rates below 3.5%, but long-term rates stayed above 5.8%, reflecting inflation expectations and fiscal deficit concerns despite the ultra-loose monetary stance.

📊

Inverted Yield Curve in 2012-2014

During the tightening cycle (2012-2014), the average term spread turned negative (-0.34%), meaning short-term T-bills yielded more than 10-year bonds. This inversion signals extreme liquidity stress — the August 2013 spike to 11.33% in 91-day T-bills was caused by the RBI's aggressive moves to defend the rupee during the "taper tantrum".

🎯

Strong Policy Transmission

The correlation between Repo Rate and 91-Day T-Bill is 0.941, confirming highly effective monetary policy transmission to the short end. However, the lower correlation with 10-Year bonds (0.815) shows long-term rates are driven by broader factors: fiscal policy, global rates, and inflation expectations.

⚡

Asymmetric Rate Transmission

Rate hikes transmit faster than cuts. In May 2022, the RBI hiked the repo by 40bps and T-bill rates surged by ~77bps that month. Conversely, during easing cycles (2019-2020), T-bill rates often took 2-3 months to fully adjust. Banks and markets are quicker to price in tightening.

🌍

Current Easing Cycle (2024-2026)

The RBI has cut the repo rate by 125 bps (from 6.50% to 5.25%) across 2025. The term spread has narrowed to ~0.52%, suggesting the market expects the easing cycle to slow. The 10-year bond at 6.78% remains elevated relative to repo, reflecting fiscal concerns and global rate uncertainty.

💰

10Y Bond Stability — the "Anchor"

The 10-Year bond rate has the lowest volatility (std: 0.76%) compared to repo (1.13%) and T-bills (1.67%). It ranges narrowly from 5.81% to 8.99% vs the repo's 4.00%-8.00%. Long-term rates act as an anchor — they don't follow short-term policy moves proportionally, reflecting India's structural premium.
